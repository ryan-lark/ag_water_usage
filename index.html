U.S. Irrigated Crop Water Usage

Water Usage for Crop Irrigation by County

Created by Ryan Lark.

Counties that did not report data are left blank, which is notably different than counties which reported 0Mgal/d. All data is given in mega-gallons (1 Mgal = 1,000,000 gallons) per day of total freshwater consumption and was sourced from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) and the U.S. Census.

In 2010, the USGS estimated irrigated agriculture accounted for 38% of the nation's freshwater withdrawals. As climate change continues to worsen and consumption continues to outcompete the replenishment of water stores, water continues to become more scarce and even more valuable. Just as there was a "green revolution" in the 1950's to increase agricultural production, there must now be a "blue revolution" to curb the rate to which water is consumed for crop irrigation.

The graph (above) depicts the average county's water consumption due to crop irrigation in the states of Washington, Kansas, and Florida. Each state was chosen to represent a different part of the U.S. Washington represents the Pacific North West, Kansas the Midwest, and Florida the South/East.
